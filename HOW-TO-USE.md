# How To Use Blueprint Factory (Owner's Guide)

Written for Jeremy — plain language, no jargon. This explains what the factory does, what's automatic,
what needs you, and how to actually make money with it in Nepal.

---

## 1. What this is, in one breath

Blueprint Factory takes a **beautiful website you point at**, studies it completely, and helps you
**rebuild it as a stunning site for one of your clients** — fast, and with quality checks the whole way.
You clone a proven 10/10 site's *structure and polish*, then swap in your client's brand, words, and
photos.

Think of it as a **workshop that turns "make me a site as nice as that one" into a real, shareable
website** in a fraction of the usual time.

---

## 2. The most important idea: you don't touch the technical stuff

Here's the mental model. Keep this in your head and everything else makes sense:

- **You are the director / the boss.** You make the decisions: which site to copy, who it's for, what
  the rules are, and whether you love the result.
- **Claude (the AI you're talking to) is the builder.** It runs all the commands, writes all the code,
  and does the heavy lifting. **You never type technical commands yourself.**
- **The "commands" you see in the docs are the builder's power tools.** They're what Claude runs under
  the hood so the work is fast and accurate. You just talk in plain English.

So when this guide mentions something like `capture` or `compare`, that's a tool **Claude runs for you**.
You don't need to memorize any of them. You describe what you want; Claude picks the right tool.

**The one phrase that starts a job** (say it to Claude in plain English, or paste this template):

```
Build a site for [Client / Company name].
Visual donor: [paste the URL of a beautiful site to copy the quality from]
Brand rules: [their colors, who it's for, the tone, and anything that must NOT appear]
```

That's it. From there, Claude drives the machine.

## 3. What's automatic vs. what needs you

### The machine does this automatically (hands-off)

| The machine… | In plain words |
| --- | --- |
| **Studies the donor site** | Takes full-page pictures on big screen, laptop, tablet, and phone; records a scroll-through video; pulls out its colors, fonts, page list, words, and images; and writes up a plan for rebuilding it. |
| **Sets the "style dial"** | Turns the donor's colors and fonts into your site's design settings, and quietly swaps any expensive/licensed fonts for free look-alikes so you never owe anyone money. |
| **Builds the pages** | Assembles the site, copying proven motion and layout pieces from its own library of patterns that already look great (instead of inventing shaky ones). |
| **Checks everything works** | Confirms the site builds, has no errors, no broken links, and is readable/accessible — automatically. |
| **Measures the match** | Compares your version to the donor **section by section** and gives a score (e.g. "91% match on desktop") plus a "fix this weakest part first" list. |
| **Captures proof** | Takes before/after screenshots and a scroll video of your version. |
| **Makes a words worksheet** | Lists the donor's text in one column so you (or Claude) can rewrite it for your client in the next column. |
| **Publishes a private preview** | Puts the site on a **shareable link** a client can open on their phone — and **refuses to publish anything still using the donor's photos** (keeps you legally safe). |
| **Keeps a dashboard** | One table showing every project you have and exactly what each one needs next. |

### You decide this (the human part — Claude does the doing, you make the calls)

| You decide… | Why it needs you |
| --- | --- |
| **Which site to copy (the "donor")** | Pick something genuinely beautiful in the client's world. The whole quality of the result comes from this choice. |
| **Who it's for + the rules** | The client's name, audience, colors, tone, and the hard "must-nots" (e.g. "women-only trekking — no male guides in photos"). |
| **The one "wow" moment** | Every site needs one signature thing that makes someone stop scrolling. You approve it. |
| **Whether the words read right** | Rewriting the donor's text into the client's real voice. Claude drafts; you sanity-check the brand feel. |
| **The real photos** | Swapping the donor's images for the client's own photos, licensed stock, or AI-generated ones. |
| **The taste call** | The final "Do I love it?" — the factory intentionally stops and asks you before anything goes live. |
| **Going live for real** | Publishing to the public internet is **never automatic**. It only happens when you say yes. |

**Rule of thumb:** the machine handles *accuracy and grunt work*; you handle *taste, brand, and
"yes."*

## 4. How a real job flows, start to finish

This is the money-making loop. Six steps.

1. **Pick a client.** A Nepal business with a weak or no website — a hotel, a trekking company, a
   restaurant, a shop, a real-estate agent, a clinic. (You already have prospect lists.)

2. **Find a gorgeous donor.** A world-class site in the *same world* as your client. A stunning hotel
   site for a hotel client, a beautiful trekking site for a trek client, etc. Higher quality donor =
   higher quality result. If you can't find one, ask Claude to research three and pick the best.

3. **Kick it off.** Tell Claude the one phrase from Section 2 with the client name, the donor URL, and
   the brand rules. Claude then automatically studies the donor and starts building.

4. **Get a preview link.** Claude builds the site (usually multiple pages), runs all the quality checks,
   and gives you a **private, shareable preview link** plus a match score and a video.

5. **Shape it in plain English.** Look at it. Tell Claude what to change — "make the hero bigger,"
   "warmer colors," "add a booking button." Do your taste pass. Approve the "wow" moment.

6. **Show the client and close.** Send them the preview link on WhatsApp/Viber. **Seeing their own
   business inside a beautiful site is what closes the deal.** Once they pay, replace the placeholder
   photos with real ones, finalize the words, and — with your yes — take it live.

The magic is step 6: you can show a prospect a *finished-looking* site of *their* business before they've
paid a rupee. That's a very strong way to sell.

## 5. The features, in plain English

You never run these — Claude does — but here's what each one is so you know what's possible.

- **Capture** — "Go study that site for me." Produces the full picture-and-plan pack of the donor.
- **Run** — "Move my project forward." Advances the site through each stage and tells you what's next.
- **Tokens** — "Match the donor's colors and fonts." Sets the site's look and swaps costly fonts for free
  ones.
- **Check** — "Make sure it actually works." Errors, broken links, accessibility, and a clean build.
- **Compare** — "How close are we to the original?" A section-by-section score and a fix list.
- **Verify** — "Run the full quality check and explain it to me." Everything at once, in plain English.
- **Copydeck** — "Give me the words to rewrite." A donor-vs-brand text worksheet.
- **Deploy (preview)** — "Give me a shareable link." A private preview; blocks if donor photos are still
  in there.
- **Status** — "Show me all my projects." One dashboard of every site and what it needs.
- **Screenshots / Motion / Beauty** — capture pictures, capture the scroll video, and run the look-and-
  feel review.

There's also a **Reference Library** — a shelf of proven, good-looking motion and layout pieces (carousels,
reveals, hero sections, etc.) that Claude copies from so your sites look crafted, not generic. Every new
site can borrow from it, and great new pieces get added back — so **each site you make makes the next one
better.**

And there's a **Green Pastures Pack** — for when a client needs a fuller *business app* (bookings, admin,
maps, etc.), not just a marketing site. Just say "with Green Pastures Pack" when you start a job.

## 6. How I'd actually use this to make money in Nepal

If this were mine, here's the plan I'd run:

**Pick one niche and own it.** Your strongest angle is **Nepal tourism** — trekking agencies, boutique
hotels, homestays, adventure operators. They live and die by how trustworthy their website looks to
foreign visitors, and most of their current sites look cheap. That's your opening. (You already have a
women-only trekking example, Alpine Bloom, and trekking prospect lists — lean into it.)

**Build a "wow" demo first, before you sell anything.** Pick one real prospect. Clone a world-class
hotel or trek site, rebrand it to *their* business, and get the preview link. Now you have a concrete
thing to show, not a promise.

**Sell with the preview link.** Your pitch is simple: *"Here's what your website could look like next
week"* — and you send them their own business in a beautiful site. That beats any brochure or price
sheet. Do this for 5–10 prospects; it's fast because the factory does the heavy work.

**Price on value, deliver fast.** Because the machine compresses the build, you can deliver in days what
agencies quote weeks for. Charge for the *result and speed*, not your hours. Offer a small "go-live +
their photos + their words" package on top.

**Reuse everything.** Every site you build teaches the factory a new pattern. Your 10th trekking site
will be faster and better than your 1st. Keep a few polished demos ready in different sectors (hotel,
restaurant, trek, retail) so you can walk into any meeting with something relevant.

**Keep production clean.** Only take a site live once the donor's photos and words are fully replaced with
the client's own or licensed/generated ones. The factory blocks you from publishing donor material — let
it. It keeps you out of trouble.

**A realistic weekly rhythm:**
- Mon–Tue: build 2–3 fresh demos for prospects (Claude does the work; you direct + taste-check).
- Wed–Thu: send preview links, follow up, take meetings.
- Fri: finalize the ones that said yes (real photos, final words, go live with your approval).

## 7. The safety rules (they protect you — don't fight them)

- **Preview is not live.** A preview link is a private share for feedback and selling. The public
  internet only happens when you explicitly approve it.
- **Donor material never ships.** The factory refuses to publish while the donor's images (and it flags
  donor words) are still in the site. Replace them first. This keeps you legally safe — you're copying
  *quality and structure*, not stealing someone's brand.
- **Nothing goes live behind your back.** Publishing, spending money, and anything permanent stops and
  asks you first.
- **The factory will tell you the truth.** If a match score is low or a section is weak, it says so.
  Trust the "fix this first" list — it's usually right.

- **"Do I need to learn commands?"** No. Talk to Claude in plain English. It runs the tools.

- **"What if I can't find a site to copy?"** Ask Claude to research three great ones in the client's
  sector and recommend the best.
- **"What if the site needs bookings/admin, not just pages?"** Say "with Green Pastures Pack."
- **"How do I make changes?"** Just describe them: "warmer," "bigger hero," "add a WhatsApp button."
