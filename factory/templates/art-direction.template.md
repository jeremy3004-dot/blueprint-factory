This file is filled in before any code is written. It commits the site to a concept and, most importantly, to one signature moment. The Build stage must honor this file. The Beauty Pass grades the site against it. If this file is vague, the site will be generic.

Keep answers short and concrete. One strong sentence beats a paragraph of hedging.

## 1. What this site is

One or two sentences. What is being built and who it is for.

## 2. The visual world

What world does this site belong to. Name it in plain words: editorial and restrained, brutalist and loud, warm and handmade, technical and precise, cinematic and dark, and so on. Avoid "modern and clean," which means nothing.

## 3. The signature moment (required)

The single interaction or visual that makes someone stop scrolling. Describe it in one sentence, concretely enough that a developer knows what to build.

Examples of the right level of detail:
- "On load, the hero headline assembles letter by letter as a 3D camera pulls back through fog."
- "As you scroll the product section, the bottle rotates and the label peels open to reveal the ingredients."
- "The cursor drags a ribbon of light that the navigation items snap onto as you move."

If you cannot name this in one sentence, the concept is not ready. Do not proceed to Build.

## 4. Motion language

How motion feels across the whole site. Pick a small set of rules, not a wish list.
- Pace: snappy, or slow and weighted.
- Easing feel: mechanical, elastic, smooth and gliding.
- Scroll feel: how heavy the Lenis smoothing should feel.
- Restraint: where motion should be quiet so the signature moment stands out.

## 5. Typography

- Display or heading typeface and why it fits the world.
- Body typeface.
- The one typographic move that gives the site character.

## 6. Color world

- Base and background.
- One or two accents.
- How dark or light the overall feel is.
- One rule about contrast or mood.

## 6b. Token translation (donor → brand)

wired into the site. Brand translation is a token operation: `app/tokens.donor.json` holds the donor-
derived baseline; edit `app/tokens.json` to the brand's values and record the diff here so structure is
kept while identity changes.

- Fonts changed from donor → brand: <e.g. Inter → Fraunces for headings>.
- Kept from donor (structure/rhythm to preserve): <type scale, spacing rhythm>.

- How sections should differ from each other so the page never feels like the same block repeated.
- First-screen plan: what a visitor sees before scrolling, and why it earns attention.
