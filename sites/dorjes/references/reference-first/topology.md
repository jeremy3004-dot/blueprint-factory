## Donor structure, top to bottom

1. Pale fixed-height masthead with compact utility controls, centered Aman wordmark and reserve action.
2. Large contained cinematic hero with centered wordmark, followed by eyebrow, headline and understated text link.
3. Asymmetric feature pair: a landscape destination story taking roughly two thirds and a portrait story taking one third.
4. Seasonal experiences: left-aligned introduction with a horizontally clipped sequence of portrait editorial cards.
5. Fine rule and centered section title.
6. Three equal portrait feature cards under “The World of Aman”.
7. Slim newsletter conversion band.
8. Dense multi-column footer and social/legal close.

## Desktop interaction model

- Header is visually quiet and remains legible over the pale masthead; menu is click-driven.
- The hero is media-led, with standard player controls in the captured state and no detected heavy animation library.
- Links use understated underline/rule feedback; cards rely on image scale/crop and editorial rhythm rather than boxed UI.
- Seasonal content is horizontally clipped to imply continuation; the production translation uses a keyboard-accessible, scroll-snap rail.
- Conversion path: hero discovery -> rooms/resort features -> experience stories -> booking/newsletter close.

## Mobile interaction model

- Compact header retains logo, menu and search/utility icons.
- Hero becomes edge-to-edge below the masthead and the copy stacks directly beneath it.
- The asymmetric feature pair becomes a single column.
- Seasonal experiences remain a horizontally scrollable rail with the next card visibly clipped.
- The final three-card world section becomes a full-width vertical sequence; footer columns compact into a denser grid.

## Typography hierarchy

- Donor display: Lyon Text Web; donor body/UI: WhitneySSm.
- Editorial headlines are modest rather than oversized (captured scale tops around 31px); the hierarchy is created through space, serif/sans contrast and casing.
- Labels are small uppercase sans with wide tracking; links are quiet and underlined.

## Color and spacing rhythm

- Dominant donor palette: warm off-white, charcoal and muted grey, with imagery providing nearly all saturation.
- Sections use generous white space and a repeated rhythm of media -> small label -> headline -> short copy -> rule link.
- Translation keeps the pale field and charcoal hierarchy while substituting Dorje's warm ivory, olive-gold, timber and lake tones.
