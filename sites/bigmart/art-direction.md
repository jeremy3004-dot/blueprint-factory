# Art Direction: bigmart

Date: 2026-06-26
Status: draft
Owner sign-off: pending

## 1. What this site is

A premium campaign concept for BigMart Nepal that makes everyday grocery shopping feel cinematic, local, and app-enabled.

## 2. The visual world

Natoora-grade food editorial translated into BigMart: spare navigation, real produce photography, centered store-selection utility, acidic campaign bands, and the app treated as the practical bridge from neighborhood store to daily basket.

## 3. The signature moment (required)

On load, a full-bleed BigMart produce hero settles behind a centered “Select your nearest BigMart” card, turning Natoora’s region selector pattern into a neighborhood-store promise.

## 4. Motion language

- Pace: quick, editorial, and calm.
- Easing feel: subtle image settle and selector rise, not cartoon bounce.
- Scroll feel: native and clean.
- Restraint: the hero reveal carries the emotion; lower sections use scale, overlap, and image rhythm instead of busy animation.

## 5. Typography

- Display type: heavy sans editorial type, close to Natoora/Farm to People confidence.
- Body type: system sans for crisp product/service explanation.
- Character move: tiny uppercase retail labels over huge compressed grocery headlines.

## 6. Color world

- Base: warm paper and ink.
- Accents: official BigMart orange, brand blue, saving red, fresh green.
- Overall feel: bright, warm, premium, and appetizing.
- Contrast rule: orange owns brand heat; blue and red appear as deliberate campaign accents, not random color blocks.

## 7. Layout system

- Grid feel: clone-derived editorial flow from Natoora: hero selector, image story, acid campaign band, seasonal cards, app CTA, mission close.
- Sections differ by composition: food-first hero, dark app/story image, acidic campaign strip, product editorial cards, app band, functional steps, mission close.
- First-screen plan: official logo, minimal nav, full-bleed BigMart produce image, and a centered store selector card.

## 9. Anti-goals

- No grocery clip-art.
- No fake route map.
- No blocky dashboard/card wall.
- No self-invented brand claims beyond public source material.
- No `READY_FOR_REVIEW` unless the screenshots look good enough to defend.
